Our 6-Step Concrete Demolition Process

1

Free On-Site Quote & Assessment

We visit your Temecula property, measure all concrete to be removed, assess thickness by examining edges or drilling test holes, check for reinforcement (rebar/wire mesh), evaluate site access for equipment, and identify any obstacles (utility lines, landscaping, structures). Written quote provided same-day or within 24 hours with clear line-item pricing. No pressure, no obligation—just honest assessment and fair pricing. Call 951-517-DIRT to schedule.

2

Site Preparation & Protection

Before breaking begins, we mark underground utilities (we recommend calling 811 DigAlert), protect adjacent landscaping with tarps or barriers, lay ground protection mats if needed to prevent lawn damage, and establish staging areas for equipment and debris piles. If your property has sprinklers, decorative rock, or delicate plants near the concrete, we take extra precautions. Professional crews respect your property—it's the difference between us and guys with rental tools.

3

Concrete Breaking & Demolition

Using hydraulic breakers mounted on skid steers, we systematically break concrete into manageable sections (typically 2-3 ft chunks). For heavily reinforced concrete, we cut rebar with grinders or torches as needed. Hand-operated jackhammers handle precision work near structures or in tight spaces where the skid steer can't reach. We work methodically from one end to the other, breaking and stacking for efficient loading. Standard 4-6 inch residential concrete breaks quickly; thicker commercial slabs take more time and impact our pricing accordingly.

4

Loading Into Dump Trailers

Broken concrete is loaded into our 14-16 ft dump trailers using the skid steer bucket. Each trailer holds 8-10 tons (roughly 4-5 cubic yards of concrete). We stack strategically to maximize each load and minimize trips to the recycling facility. For projects in tight access areas where the skid steer can't reach, we wheelbarrow chunks to the staging area then bucket-load. A standard 500 sq ft driveway typically requires 2-3 trailer loads depending on thickness.

5

Hauling & Recycling

All concrete is hauled to licensed recycling facilities in Riverside County where it's crushed and processed into aggregate for road base, new construction, and landscaping rock. Concrete is 100% recyclable and disposal fees are included in our quotes—you never pay separate dump charges. We make as many trips as needed to clear all debris from your property. Trip frequency depends on project size and distance to the facility (most Temecula jobs are 15-25 minutes from recycling centers).

6

Final Grading, Cleanup & Inspection

After all concrete is removed, we rake and level the exposed dirt, remove any remaining chips or small debris, grade the area flat (or to match adjacent grade), and sweep driveways/streets clean of concrete dust. If you're pouring new concrete soon, we can compact the base to prevent future settling. If landscaping is going in, we leave the soil loose for planting. Final walk-through with you to ensure complete satisfaction before we leave. Property left cleaner than we found it—that's the Temecula Valley Dirt Co. standard.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does concrete removal cost in Temecula?

Concrete removal in Temecula costs $2-$6 per square foot depending on thickness, reinforcement, access, and project size. Standard residential driveways (400-500 sq ft, 4-6 inches thick) typically run $2,500-$4,000 including all breaking, loading, hauling, and disposal. Smaller projects like walkways or small patios start around $800. Large projects over 1,000 sq ft can reach $6,000-$10,000.

Factors affecting cost: concrete thickness (4" vs 8" makes a big difference), reinforcement type (wire mesh vs heavy rebar), site access (equipment accessible vs hand-labor only), condition (cracked breaks faster than solid), and whether base material under the concrete also needs removal. We provide free written quotes after seeing your specific project. Call 951-517-DIRT for accurate pricing.

How long does concrete removal take?

Most concrete removal projects in Temecula take 1-2 days to complete. A standard residential driveway (400-500 sq ft, 4-6 inches thick) is typically finished in one full working day including breaking, loading, hauling, and site cleanup. We arrive in the morning, systematically break and remove all concrete, make 2-3 dump runs, and clean up before leaving.

Larger projects (800+ sq ft), heavily reinforced slabs with extensive rebar, or multiple separate areas may require 2 days. Projects requiring precision saw-cutting, post-tension cable handling, or extreme tight-access hand labor can extend to 2-3 days. Timeline is discussed during the free quote—we give you accurate expectations upfront, not vague estimates.

What equipment do you use for concrete removal?

Do I need a permit for concrete removal in Temecula?

Removal-only projects typically don't require permits in Temecula. You're simply demolishing and hauling away existing concrete without constructing anything new or altering drainage. However, if you're replacing the concrete, expanding the footprint, or doing site work that affects stormwater drainage, the City of Temecula Building & Safety Department may require permits.

Specific scenarios: removing a driveway to pour a new one (same footprint) typically doesn't need permits; expanding a driveway or changing its slope usually does; removing concrete near property lines may require verification you're not encroaching; projects affecting drainage or adding impervious surface may trigger stormwater requirements. We can guide you through Temecula's requirements and connect you with permit resources if needed. When in doubt, call Temecula Building & Safety at (951) 694-6410.

What do you do with the broken concrete?

All concrete is hauled to licensed recycling facilities in Riverside County where it's crushed and processed into recycled aggregate. This crushed concrete is used as road base for new construction, fill material for development projects, and decorative landscaping rock. Concrete is 100% recyclable—none goes to landfills.

Environmental benefit: recycling concrete reduces the need for virgin quarried rock, decreases landfill waste, and lowers the carbon footprint of new construction. Riverside County has excellent concrete recycling infrastructure with multiple facilities accepting residential demolition concrete. Disposal fees are included in our quotes—you never pay separately for dump runs or tipping fees. We handle all hauling, weighing, and recycling documentation.

Can you remove concrete with rebar?

Yes, we regularly remove heavily reinforced concrete including rebar (reinforcing bar), wire mesh, post-tension cables, and fiber-reinforced concrete throughout Temecula. Rebar adds complexity because it must be cut with angle grinders, reciprocating saws, or torches before chunks can be removed. This increases labor time compared to unreinforced concrete.

Reinforcement types: wire mesh (most common in driveways, breaks relatively easily), standard rebar (typically #3 or #4, requires cutting), heavy rebar (#5-#8, common in commercial slabs, very time-intensive), and post-tension cables (found in some foundations, require specialized cutting to prevent cable whip). We identify reinforcement type during the quote process and price accordingly. Heavy rebar can add $200-800 to a project depending on density and thickness.

Will concrete removal damage my landscaping?

We take precautions to minimize landscaping damage. Skid steers and trailers stay on driveways or hard surfaces when possible. For areas where equipment must cross lawns, we use ground protection mats to distribute weight and prevent rutting. Hand tools are used near delicate plants, irrigation systems, and decorative features.

Realistic expectations: some minor lawn damage from equipment traffic is sometimes unavoidable, especially on saturated ground or steep grades. Tire tracks typically recover within 2-3 weeks or can be reseeded easily. We don't drive equipment through flower beds, over irrigation manifolds, or across decorative rock areas if avoidable. If your property has extensive landscaping adjacent to the concrete, discuss protection measures during the quote—we'll plan equipment routing carefully.

How thick is my concrete?

Most residential driveways in Temecula are 4-6 inches thick. Standard residential patios are typically 4 inches. Older driveways (1970s-1980s) may be only 3-4 inches and often lack reinforcement. Newer construction (1990s-present) tends to be 4-6 inches with wire mesh or light rebar. Commercial driveways, RV pads, and equipment areas are often 6-8 inches thick with heavier reinforcement.

How we measure: if the concrete edge is exposed (at landscaping or garage threshold), we can measure directly. If edges are buried, we drill a small test hole with a hammer drill to determine thickness. For large projects where thickness is uncertain, we may break a test section first to assess reinforcement and adjust pricing if dramatically different than estimated. Thickness significantly affects labor time and disposal weight, so accurate measurement ensures fair pricing for both parties.

What's the difference between concrete removal and demolition?

The terms are used interchangeably in the industry. Concrete removal, concrete demolition, concrete demo, and concrete breakup all refer to the same service: breaking up existing concrete, loading it, and hauling it away. There's no technical distinction—it's regional language preference.

Complete service includes: breaking concrete into moveable chunks using hydraulic breakers or jackhammers, loading broken pieces into dump trailers, hauling to recycling facilities, disposal/recycling fees, raking and leveling exposed dirt, removing all debris and small chips, and sweeping driveways/streets clean. Whether you search "concrete removal Temecula" or "concrete demolition Temecula," you'll find the same service providers offering the same work.

Can you remove concrete in tight spaces?

Yes, we specialize in tight-access concrete removal throughout Temecula. For areas inaccessible to skid steers—narrow side yards (under 8 ft), gated backyards with small gates (36-48 inches), or areas with low overhead clearance—we use hand-operated pneumatic jackhammers, electric breakers, or manual demolition tools. Broken concrete is wheelbarrowed to accessible staging areas then loaded mechanically.

Tight access pricing: hand-labor-only projects cost more per square foot because they're slower and more physically demanding. A driveway accessible to our skid steer might cost $3/sq ft; the same driveway requiring all hand labor could be $5/sq ft. We assess access during the free quote and price accordingly. Even in challenging access situations, professional experience means we work efficiently—we've removed concrete from backyards with 36-inch gates, elevated hillside patios, and crawl-space-height areas.
