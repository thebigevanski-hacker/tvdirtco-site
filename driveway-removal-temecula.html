How Driveway Removal Works

1

Free Quote & Site Assessment

We visit your Temecula property, measure the driveway, check thickness (we can core-sample if needed), assess access, and provide a written quote. Most quotes given same-day or within 24 hours. No obligation to proceed.

2

Concrete Breaking

Using skid-steer-mounted hydraulic breakers and hand-operated jackhammers, we systematically break up the concrete into moveable chunks (typically 2-3 ft sections). We work from one end to the other, breaking the driveway into pieces our equipment can lift.

3

Loading & Hauling

Broken concrete is loaded into our dump trailers using a skid steer. We make multiple trips to the recycling facility—each trailer holds about 8-10 tons. A standard 400 sq ft driveway typically requires 2-3 loads. All hauling is included in your quote.

4

Base Material Removal (Optional)

If you're pouring a new driveway, we can also remove the old base rock/gravel underneath the concrete. This ensures a clean, properly compacted sub-base for your new driveway. Additional cost applies if base removal is needed.

5

Final Grading & Cleanup

Once all concrete is removed, we rake and level the area, remove any remaining chips or debris, and grade the dirt flat. If you're not pouring immediately, we can also compact the soil to prevent settling. Your property is left clean and ready.

Driveway Removal FAQs

How much does driveway removal cost in Temecula?

Driveway removal in Temecula costs $1,800-$6,000 for most residential projects. A standard 2-car driveway (400-500 sq ft, 4-6 inches thick) typically runs $2,500-$4,000 including breaking, loading, hauling, and recycling. Smaller single-car driveways start around $1,800. Large circular drives or heavily reinforced commercial-grade driveways can exceed $6,000. We provide free written quotes after seeing your specific driveway—thickness, size, and access all affect final pricing.

How long does it take to remove a driveway?

Most driveway removals in Temecula take 1 full day (6-8 hours) to complete. We arrive in the morning, break up the concrete, load it into dump trailers, make 2-3 trips to the recycling facility, and clean up the site before we leave. Larger driveways (800+ sq ft) or driveways with extensive rebar may take 2 days. Weather rarely affects timeline since concrete work isn't impacted by light rain.

Can you remove just part of my driveway?

Yes, we regularly do partial driveway removals for garage expansions, property line adjustments, or damaged section replacement. We use concrete saws to cut clean, straight lines separating what's being removed from what stays. This prevents cracking in the remaining driveway. Partial removals cost less than full removals but typically have higher per-square-foot rates due to the precision cutting required.

What happens to the old driveway concrete?

We haul all driveway concrete to licensed recycling facilities in Riverside County where it's crushed into aggregate. This recycled concrete is used as road base, fill material, and landscaping rock. Concrete is 100% recyclable and keeps thousands of tons out of landfills. Recycling fees are included in our pricing—you don't pay extra for environmentally responsible disposal.

Do I need a permit to remove my driveway in Temecula?

Removal-only typically doesn't require a permit in Temecula. You're demolishing existing concrete without changing drainage, adding structures, or altering the property. However, if you're replacing the driveway with a new one, expanding the footprint, or doing site work that affects stormwater flow, the City of Temecula may require permits. We can help you determine what's needed for your specific project.
