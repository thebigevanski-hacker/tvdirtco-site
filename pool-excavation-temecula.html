Pool Types We Excavate

Gunite / Shotcrete Pools

The most popular pool type in Temecula. Gunite pools require precise excavation with clean, straight walls and exact depth control. We work closely with your pool contractor to ensure the excavation matches engineering plans perfectly, including proper slopes for drains and equipment placement.

  • ✓ Standard rectangle and freeform shapes
  • ✓ Infinity edge and vanishing edge pools
  • ✓ Attached spa excavation
  • ✓ Beach entry and tanning ledge areas

Fiberglass Pools

Fiberglass pool shells require excavation slightly larger than the shell dimensions to allow for backfill and leveling. We excavate to manufacturer specifications, create a level sand base, and ensure proper drainage around the shell perimeter.

  • ✓ Precise measurements for shell fit
  • ✓ Gravel base preparation
  • ✓ Proper overdig for backfill access
  • ✓ Coordination with crane placement

Vinyl Liner Pools

Vinyl liner pools use a polymer or steel wall structure. We excavate to exact dimensions with square corners and level walls, then compact the floor to prevent settling under the liner.

  • ✓ Square corners for wall panels
  • ✓ Level floor compaction
  • ✓ Exact depth to match liner
  • ✓ Smooth walls for panel installation

Our Pool Excavation Process

1

Site Assessment & Planning

We review your pool plans, assess site access, identify utility lines, and determine the best approach for your property. For hillside lots common in Temecula, we plan equipment positioning and soil removal logistics.

2

Utility Marking & Protection

We coordinate with Underground Service Alert (811) and mark all gas, electric, water, sewer, and irrigation lines. Any lines running through the pool area are carefully exposed and protected during excavation.

3

Precision Excavation

Using laser levels and GPS technology, we excavate to exact depth, shape, and grade per engineering plans. For gunite pools, we create clean walls for rebar and plumbing installation. For fiberglass, we ensure perfect shell fit.

4

Soil Removal & Cleanup

We haul away all excavated soil (typically 50-150 cubic yards depending on pool size), clean the work area, and coordinate with your pool contractor for next steps. Your yard is left ready for pool construction to begin.
