Damage Waiver Policy

Last Updated: February 6, 2026

What is the Damage Waiver?

The optional Damage Waiver (DW) is an additional charge (12% of your rental subtotal) that reduces your financial liability for certain types of damage to rented equipment during the rental period.

This is NOT insurance. It is a waiver that limits your responsibility for covered damage when you meet all rental agreement terms.

Cost

12% of equipment and add-ons subtotal

Examples:

  • $1,000 rental → $120 damage waiver
  • $2,500 rental → $300 damage waiver
  • $500 rental → $60 damage waiver

What the Damage Waiver COVERS

If you purchase the Damage Waiver, your liability is reduced to $0 (deductible waived) for:

  • Accidental damage during normal, proper use of equipment
  • Mechanical breakdowns not caused by misuse or negligence
  • Scratches, dents, and cosmetic damage from jobsite operations
  • Hydraulic hose damage from normal wear
  • Tire/track damage from normal use on approved terrain
  • Minor operational accidents (e.g., backing into object, tipping on uneven ground)

With Damage Waiver: You pay $0 for these covered damages (up to full equipment value).

What the Damage Waiver DOES NOT COVER

The Damage Waiver does NOT protect you from liability for:

  • Theft or loss of equipment (full replacement cost applies)
  • Gross negligence or reckless operation
  • Intentional damage or abuse
  • Operating equipment while impaired (drugs/alcohol)
  • Use outside rental agreement terms (e.g., prohibited uses)
  • Overloading beyond rated capacity
  • Failure to report damage or issues promptly
  • Missing parts, attachments, or accessories at return
  • Vandalism while equipment is unsecured
  • Environmental contamination (fuel/oil spills not cleaned up)
  • Third-party liability (injury to persons or property damage to others)

Without Damage Waiver or for excluded claims: You remain 100% liable for all costs.

Do I Need the Damage Waiver?

Consider PURCHASING if:

  • You don't have commercial insurance covering rented equipment
  • Your insurance deductible is high ($1,000+)
  • You're operating in challenging conditions (tight spaces, slopes)
  • You're new to operating this type of equipment
  • You want peace of mind and budget protection

Consider DECLINING if:

  • You have comprehensive commercial insurance with low deductible
  • Your Certificate of Insurance (COI) covers rented equipment
  • You're an experienced operator with excellent safety record
  • You want to minimize upfront costs

Claims Process

If damage occurs during your rental:

  1. Report immediately - Call us as soon as damage occurs
  2. Document the incident - Photos, description, circumstances
  3. Do not continue using damaged equipment
  4. We inspect and assess damage
  5. If Damage Waiver was purchased and damage is covered: $0 charge to you
  6. If excluded or no waiver: You pay repair/replacement costs

Important Notes

  • Damage Waiver is optional - you are never required to purchase it
  • Purchasing the waiver does not excuse you from proper care and operation of equipment
  • All terms of the Rental Agreement still apply
  • Waiver is non-refundable once rental period begins
  • Third-party liability (injury/property damage) requires your insurance
