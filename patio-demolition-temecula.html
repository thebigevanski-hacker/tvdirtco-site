Professional Equipment for Patio Demolition

🔨 Variable-Force Breakers

Our hydraulic breakers have adjustable impact force—full power for thick concrete, reduced force near pools or structures. This control prevents damage to pool coping, tile, or adjacent hardscape. We can dial power down to 40% when working within 2 feet of your pool edge.

🚜 Compact Equipment

Our skid steers fit through 48-inch gates and operate in confined backyards. We use mini-excavators for patios inaccessible to larger equipment. Ground protection mats distribute weight when crossing lawns.

🛠️ Precision Hand Tools

Electric and pneumatic jackhammers for work within 12 inches of pools, planters, or outdoor kitchens. Concrete saws for cutting clean lines. We bring the right tool for each phase.

📏 Laser Levels

For partial patio removals, we use laser levels to establish cut lines and verify elevations. This ensures remaining concrete stays level and properly sloped for drainage.

Patio Types We Remove

🏊 Pool Decks

Complete pool deck removal is common where homeowners upgrade to pavers or natural stone. We work carefully around pool edges, coping, and tile. Equipment never approaches closer than 3 feet—hand tools handle perimeter work.

🏗️ Raised Patios

Raised entertainment areas on fill or retaining walls. We demo the concrete surface plus address support structures. If walls stay, we cut flush. If walls go, we demo everything and regrade.

🪑 Sunken Patios

Popular in 1970s-80s homes. We break the slab plus backfill the void with compacted soil to bring grade back to yard level. Final grading prepares for new installation.

🎨 Decorative Concrete

Stamped, exposed aggregate, acid-stained, or colored concrete. The decorative finish is only 1/8 inch deep—it all breaks and hauls the same as plain gray concrete.
